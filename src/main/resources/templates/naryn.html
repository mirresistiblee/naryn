    Land of Nomads

    High mountains, green meadows, steep passes, and crystal-clear air – these are the images that come to mind when mentioning the Naryn Region. Most of the region’s territory, about 95%, is located at an altitude of over 1000 meters. Due to its climate, it is the coolest region in the country: the winters are long and cold, while the summers are warm but quite short. However, it is here that you can see vast spaces with lush, incredibly rich grass, which makes it ideal for raising the best livestock. The Naryn Region has always been the land of nomads who lived in harmony with the surrounding nature, constantly moving from winter pastures to summer ones. Today, it is still possible to live in a yurt on the high mountain pastures (jailoos) and experience the nomadic lifestyle, while also exploring the local ecology and feeling the freedom and unity with nature. In this region, you can witness true natural wonders. It is the source of the longest and most powerful river in the country, the Naryn, which holds enormous hydroelectric potential. There are many large and small glaciers, covering over 1400 square kilometers, which store significant amounts of freshwater. High in the mountains, you can see several lakes, whose breathtaking beauty will leave you in awe. The most famous and largest of them are Son-Kul and Chatyr-Kul lakes.
     
    Summer

     In summer, Naryn is a great place to experience life on one of the famous jailoos (Sarala-Saz, Jumgal, Jamankechki, Kilemche), where you can learn about the life, customs, and culture of the herders. Here, you can taste the national drink of fermented mare’s milk, kumis, which has wonderful healing properties. Kumis therapy is widely practiced, where people come to jailoos for extended periods to improve their health and cure illnesses. Horseback and hiking trips in nature also provide tourists with a peaceful escape from the bustle of the city. In the Naryn mountains, many roads with long descents and ascents attract cycling enthusiasts. While cycling along mountain paths is no easy feat, travelers are rewarded with breathtaking views of the stunning landscapes from above. The best views are from the passes of Dolon, Kalmak-Ashuu, Moldo-Ashuu, and Kyzart. For thrill-seekers, the Naryn River offers great opportunities for rafting in its fast, powerful flow. Those interested in cultural and educational tourism will gain new knowledge while traveling through the Naryn Region. One of the branches of the Great Silk Road, the medieval monuments of Tash-Rabat and Koshoy-Korgon, will reveal their secrets to travelers. 

    Winter

     In some parts of Naryn, the winter can be extremely cold, with temperatures dropping to -50°C. As a result, winter tourism is only possible in the northern part of the region. Skiers and snowboarders will experience the thrill of freeride, skiing down wild mountain slopes off prepared tracks. The untouched snow provides a chance to be one with the elements and fully enjoy the possibilities of snowboarding and alpine skiing.
